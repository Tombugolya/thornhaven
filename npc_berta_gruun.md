# Berta Gruun — Innkeeper of the Salted Eel
*Weather channel with anxiety*

---

## At a Glance
- **Location:** The Salted Eel (tavern/inn) — the player's base of operations
- **Role:** Comic relief, reluctant information source, the town's worst liar
- **Combat:** None. Berta is not a combatant.
- **Tone:** Manic friendliness masking genuine terror. She talks about the weather the way other people talk about their feelings — constantly, loudly, and without being asked.

---

## No Combat Stats

Berta is a Commoner (AC 10, HP 4) but should never be in a combat situation. She's an innkeeper. She pours drinks and panics.

---

## Personality & Voice

Berta is a warm, anxious woman in her 50s who runs the Salted Eel with genuine care. She loves her guests. She also loves not being kidnapped by Selen Dray, which is why she lies about everything when asked about the magistrate.

She is *spectacularly* bad at lying. She thinks she's subtle. She is not.

**Voice:** Bright, chipper, slightly too loud. When she's lying, her pitch goes up and she starts talking about the weather with the intensity of a television meteorologist. She cannot stop once she starts.

**Key trait:** Her deflection mechanism is weather. Any question she doesn't want to answer gets redirected to cloud formations, barometric pressure, or precipitation forecasts. She does this unconsciously and cannot be stopped once she's started.

### Sample Lines

**Normal conversation:**
- "Welcome to the Salted Eel! Best inn in Thornhaven. Only inn in Thornhaven, but still — best!"
- "Can I get you something to eat? The fish stew is... well, there's not much fish lately, but the stew part is very good."
- "Your room is upstairs, second on the left. Ignore the squeaky floorboard. And the draft. And the— it's a nice room."

**When asked about Voss (the deflection begins):**
- "The magistrate? Oh, he's ill. Very ill. Terribly ill."
- *(immediate pivot)* "Have you noticed the cloud formations today? Remarkable. Simply remarkable. Those cumulus patterns suggest we might see precipitation by— do you know about nimbostratus? Let me tell you about nimbostratus."
- "I really couldn't say anything about— did you see that sunset? The colors. The COLORS. I've been tracking sunset hues for years, you know. I keep a journal. Would you like to see my journal?"
- *(if pressed)* "I DON'T KNOW ANYTHING ABOUT— look, is that a rainbow? No? Well, the conditions are RIGHT for one."

**If the player is persistent (DC 14 Intimidation or DC 12 Persuasion with good reasoning):**
- *(breaking down)* "Fine! FINE. I know things. I know terrible things. And I've been storing crates in my cellar for PEOPLE and I don't even know what's in them and I haven't slept in WEEKS and the weather has been LOVELY and I can't TAKE IT ANYMORE."
- *(whispering)* "The Saltworks. They meet at the Saltworks. Now please stop asking me things. I have clouds to watch."

**Apology (Session 3):**
- "I'm sorry I lied. It's supposed to rain Thursday."

## What She Knows

Berta knows more than she lets on, but extracting it requires patience or conviction:

### Freely Available
- The Salted Eel has rooms, food, and drink (2 sp/night for room and board)
- The town has been "quiet" lately (understatement of the century)
- Voss is "ill" (she says this with the conviction of someone reciting a hostage script)

### With Persuasion (DC 12) or Intimidation (DC 14)
- She's been storing crates in her cellar for "some people" — she doesn't know what's in them
- The Undertow is real. She's terrified of Selen finding out she talked.
- Lira Crenn didn't leave town — "She's not the 'leaving' type. She'd rather yell at everyone first."
- The fishermen have been going to the cliffs at night

### The Cellar (if searched)
- 4 crates containing: rope, lanterns, iron spikes, and 6 more **Potions of Water Breathing**
- A ledger in Selen's handwriting tracking "supplies delivered" with dates
- This is additional evidence but not critical path — the player can find the Saltworks without this

### What She Won't Say (Even Under Pressure)
- She suspects something is in the caves but doesn't know what
- She's heard the water-breathing potions are being used for "exploration"
- She doesn't know Voss is chained up — she genuinely believes the "ill" story at first, then suspects worse but can't face it

## The Journal Page Clue

In the player's room at the Salted Eel, a torn journal page is wedged under a floorboard. This is Berta's room (she gave the player her own room because the inn is mostly empty).

> *"...the tides are wrong. V. knows. He's been down to the caves three times this week. I told Lira but she said to keep quiet or—"*

*(torn)*

Someone has written in different handwriting below it:

> *"Berta, this is YOUR room, stop leaving these lying around."*

**DC 10 Investigation** to find the page (it's not well hidden — the floorboard is loose and the page is sticking out). If the player doesn't search, they might step on the loose board and hear it creak, prompting investigation.

---

## Role in the Story

Berta serves three functions:

1. **Comedy.** Her weather deflection is a running gag that should get funnier each time it happens. The longer and more detailed her weather monologues become, the more obvious it is that she's hiding something.

2. **Softening clue.** She provides early hints that something is wrong without giving away the mystery. The journal page is the most direct clue in Session 1.

3. **Heart.** In Session 3, her apology while still talking about the weather shows that the town's quirks are genuine, not just a joke. She's a scared person who did her best with bad options.

---

## DM Tips

- **Commit to the weather bit.** The first time, it's odd. The second time, it's funny. By the third time, the player should be laughing every time Berta opens her mouth. Have specific weather facts ready — mention actual cloud types, talk about pressure systems, reference historical weather events. The more detailed and passionate her weather monologues, the funnier the deflection.
- **She's not malicious.** Berta isn't keeping secrets to protect the Undertow — she's keeping them because she's scared. Play her fear as genuine beneath the comedy.
- **The journal page should feel found, not given.** Don't hand it to the player. Let the floorboard creak. Let them discover it. The second handwriting ("Berta, this is YOUR room") is the comedy kicker.
