# Undertow Lieutenant
*The only competent member of the Undertow*

---

## At a Glance
- **Used in:** Saltworks rescue (Session 2, Part 1)
- **Role:** The one true believer among the bumbling Undertow — genuinely dangerous
- **Tone:** This is where the humor starts to crack. She's serious, and it's unsettling because nobody else is.
- **Name suggestion:** Vara Selk (or leave unnamed — "the lieutenant")

## Spell Quick Reference

### Cantrips (at will)
- **Sacred Flame:** DEX save DC 11 or 1d8 radiant damage. No cover bonus on save. 60 ft range.
- **Thaumaturgy:** Minor sensory effects — booming voice, flickering flames, tremors. Great for intimidation RP.
- **Light:** Touch an object, it glows for 1 hour. 20 ft bright light, 20 ft dim.

### 1st Level (4 slots)
- **Command** (1 action, 60 ft): WIS save DC 11. One word command: *Flee, Grovel, Drop, Halt, Approach*. Target spends next turn obeying.
- **Inflict Wounds** (1 action, touch): Melee spell attack +3. **3d10 necrotic damage.** Her big hitter.
- **Shield of Faith** (bonus action, 60 ft, concentration, 10 min): Target gets +2 AC. Cast on self or Selen if present.

### 2nd Level (3 slots)
- **Hold Person** (1 action, 60 ft, concentration, 1 min): WIS save DC 11. Target is paralyzed. Repeats save at end of each turn. **Dangerous — melee attacks against paralyzed targets auto-crit.**
- **Spiritual Weapon** (bonus action, 60 ft, 1 min): Spectral weapon appears, attacks as bonus action each turn. +4 to hit, 1d8+2 force damage. **No concentration.** Flavored as a spectral trident with the spiral wave symbol.

## Personality & Voice

She's the exception that proves the rule. While the rest of the Undertow is comically inept, the lieutenant is a true believer. She found something real in Selen's vision — purpose, belonging, a cause — and she won't let it go easily.

**Voice:** Calm, measured, slightly condescending. She doesn't shout. She doesn't panic. That's what makes her scary next to the buffoons.

**Sample lines:**
- "You don't understand what we've found. None of you do."
- "Selen sees what this town could be. I intend to help her build it."
- *(to the thugs, who are panicking)* "Stop. Breathe. Do your jobs."
- "The altar chose us. You can join us, or you can be forgotten with the rest of this dying coast."
- *(if defeated)* "This changes nothing. The sea remembers."

## Combat Tactics

**She's the real threat in the Saltworks fight.** Run her smart.

### Priority Order
1. **Round 1:** Cast **Spiritual Weapon** (bonus action) + **Hold Person** on the PC (action). If Hold Person lands, the thugs get auto-crits in melee.
2. **Round 2:** Spiritual Weapon attack (bonus action) + **Inflict Wounds** on a held/adjacent target (3d10 necrotic — can drop a level 3 PC).
3. **Round 3+:** **Sacred Flame** at range if melee is too dangerous. **Command: Flee** to separate PC from Maren.
4. **If losing:** Cast **Shield of Faith** on herself and fight defensively, trying to flee toward the caves to warn Selen.

### Key Behaviors
- She positions behind the thugs, using them as a screen
- She targets the PC over Maren (correctly identifies the bigger threat)
- She does NOT surrender. Dark Devotion means she fights to unconsciousness.
- If the thugs flee, she calls them cowards but doesn't chase them

### Warning for DM
**Hold Person + Inflict Wounds is potentially lethal for a level 3 PC.** If the PC fails the WIS save, consider having the lieutenant use Command or Sacred Flame instead of Inflict Wounds to avoid an unfun one-round KO. Save Inflict Wounds for when the PC is healthy and it'll feel dramatic rather than cheap.

## What She Knows

She won't share willingly, but if somehow compelled:
- The altar is ancient — older than Thornhaven, older than the kingdom
- Selen believes it can bring the fish back, restore the trade routes, save the town
- The lieutenant believes it can do more than that ("The sea has a will. We've heard it.")
- Voss is alive, chained in the caves. She thinks that's "unfortunate but necessary."
- She genuinely doesn't care about gold. This is about power and purpose.
