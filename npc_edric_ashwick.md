## Personality & Voice

Edric is 19, maybe 20. He's Maren's younger brother — the kid who always felt like he was living in her shadow. He joined the Undertow because Selen told him he could make something of himself. He believed her because he wanted to.

He's not evil. He's not even particularly brave. He's a young man who wanted to matter and got in way too deep.

**Voice:** Eager, slightly too loud, apologetic. When Maren shows up, all the bravado evaporates instantly.

### Sample Lines

**Before Maren appears:**
- *(trying to sound tough)* "You shouldn't have come down here. The Undertow doesn't take kindly to—"
- *(sotto voce to a thug)* "Do I sound intimidating? Be honest."

**When he sees Maren:**
- "Maren, I can explain—"
- "It's not what it looks like! Well, it IS what it looks like, but—"
- "I was going to tell you. I was! ...Eventually."

**If talked down:**
- "I just wanted to do something that mattered. Selen said I could help save the town."
- "I know it was stupid. You don't have to say it."
- *(to Maren)* "Please don't tell Mum."

**After the fight:**
- "I'm sorry, Maren. I really am."
- "She made it sound so important. Like we were heroes."

## Combat Tactics

Edric doesn't want to fight. Especially not once Maren is there.

### Behavior
- **Round 1:** He holds his action, looking between Selen and Maren. He doesn't attack unless directly threatened or Selen orders him.
- **Round 2+:** Fights halfheartedly. Attacks with his spear but doesn't pursue retreating enemies.
- **Below half HP (5 HP):** Drops his weapon and surrenders, especially if Maren is present or the player invokes her.
- **If Maren talks to him (no action required from Maren, but costs the player an Action — DC 13 Persuasion, advantage if invoking Maren):** He surrenders immediately, regardless of HP.

### DM Note
Edric should feel like a rescue, not a kill. The player should want to save him. If the player attacks him aggressively, Maren calls out — "Don't! He's my— he's just a kid!"

Low HP (11) means he can go down fast if targeted. Consider having him drop to 0 and stabilize rather than die, unless the player is specifically trying to be lethal. His death would fundamentally change the tone.

## What He Knows

Edric knows surprisingly little — Selen kept the inner circle small:
- He was recruited 2 months ago by Todd (yes, that Todd)
- He's been standing guard, running errands, and attending Tuesday meetings
- He knows Voss is in the caves ("Selen said he was being 'kept safe.' I... I chose to believe that.")
- He knows about the altar but hasn't been in the flooded chamber
- He has no idea what the altar actually does ("Selen said it would bring the fish back. That's all I know.")
- He doesn't know about Lira being held prisoner ("She's WHAT? I thought she left town!")

## The Maren Dynamic

This is the emotional core of the confrontation. Key beats:

1. **The reveal:** Edric is standing with Selen's group. Maren freezes. She suspected, but knowing and seeing are different.
2. **Maren's silence:** She doesn't attack in Round 1. She's processing.
3. **The player's role:** The PC can talk Edric down mid-fight. This is the intended resolution.
4. **After:** Maren is furious with Edric but also protective. "He's an idiot, but he's *my* idiot."

### Maren's Lines During Confrontation
- *(seeing Edric)* "...Oh. Oh, you *absolute* —"
- *(if Edric fights)* "Edric Ashwick, you put that spear down RIGHT NOW."
- *(if Edric surrenders)* "We are going to have a VERY long conversation when this is over."
- *(to the player, quietly)* "Please don't hurt him."

---

## Resolution (Session 3)

Maren asks the player to advocate for leniency for Edric. This is a genuine moral choice.

**Arguments for leniency:**
- He's young, he was manipulated, he didn't know the full scope
- He surrendered willingly
- Maren vouches for him
- He's genuinely remorseful

**Arguments against:**
- He helped kidnap the magistrate (even if he didn't know)
- He stood guard while Lira was imprisoned
- Being young and stupid isn't a legal defense
- Setting a precedent matters

There is no right answer. Let the player sit with it.
