# Lira Crenn — Alchemist
*Angry scientist, reluctant witness*

---

## At a Glance
- **Found in:** Saltworks basement, locked room (Session 2, Part 1)
- **Role:** Prisoner and exposition source. She has critical information about the altar and the caves.
- **Combat:** Non-combatant. She can provide potions and support but does not fight.
- **Tone:** Furious, sarcastic, scientifically precise even when ranting. She's been imprisoned for three weeks and has had nothing to do but stew.

---

## No Combat Stats

Lira is not a fighter. If absolutely necessary, treat her as a **Commoner** (AC 10, HP 4, no meaningful attacks). She will not enter combat willingly.

What she CAN do:
- Provide potions (see below)
- Identify alchemical substances
- Offer medical assistance (Medicine +5 if you need a check)
- Throw insults with devastating accuracy

---

## Personality & Voice

Lira is a trained alchemist — sharp, precise, and absolutely livid about her current circumstances. She helped the Undertow voluntarily at first (she was curious about the altar's chemical properties) and is deeply embarrassed about it.

**Voice:** Rapid-fire, sardonic, technical. She uses scientific terminology when angry, which is always. Think: a tenured professor who got kidnapped mid-lecture and is furious about the interruption.

**Key trait:** She's the one who understands the actual danger. The thugs are jokes, Selen is deluded, but Lira has *seen* what the altar does up close, and it scared her. When she talks about the caves, the sarcasm drops.

### Sample Lines

**When rescued:**
- "Oh wonderful, a rescue. Only three weeks late. I've been subsisting on salt crackers and resentment."
- "Do you know what it's like being imprisoned by people who can't even organize a snack rotation? There were MINUTES about it. MEETING MINUTES."
- *(looking at the player)* "Please tell me you're competent. I've had my fill of the alternative."
- "I need a bath, a meal that isn't salt, and to never see another spiral wave symbol as long as I live."

**About her involvement (embarrassed):**
- "Yes, I helped them. Voluntarily. At first. Because I'm a scientist and the altar's mineralogical composition was — look, it was *interesting*, alright? Don't judge me."
- "I brewed the water-breathing potions. I translated some of the inscriptions. And then I told Selen she was playing with things she didn't understand, and suddenly I'm in a basement."
- "My professional curiosity has, on occasion, outpaced my professional judgment. This was one of those occasions."

**About the altar (serious — the sarcasm drops here):**
- "But the thing in the caves? That's not a joke. The water moves on its own down there. The inscriptions... I couldn't read them, but they made my teeth ache. Whatever's sealed down there was sealed for a reason."
- "The altar isn't treasure. It's not a power source. It's a containment system. Someone — someone very old, very powerful, and very frightened — built it to keep something *in*."
- "I touched the stone once. Just once. I felt cold. Not temperature — *emotional* cold. Loneliness. Like the sea in winter when there's nothing alive for miles. I don't want to feel that again."
- "The water around the altar doesn't move right. Drop something in and it doesn't ripple. It... *absorbs*. I've never seen anything like it and I hope I never do again."

**About the other NPCs:**
- *(about Selen)* "She's not evil. She's just aggressively delusional about her importance. She thinks she's saving the town. She's going to drown it."
- *(about Voss)* "Harlan tried to stop them. He's either in the caves or... look, Selen isn't violent. She's just *aggressively delusional* about her importance."
- *(about the Undertow)* "They're not a cult. They're a HOA with delusions of grandeur. The cult fanatic — the lieutenant — SHE'S the problem. She actually believes."
- *(about Edric, if mentioned)* "The young one? He's harmless. Puppyish. I don't think he knows what he's involved in."

## What She Knows

Lira is the best source of *specific, technical* information:

### The Altar
- Ancient — she estimates thousands of years old based on mineral deposits
- Made of a stone she can't identify. It doesn't match any local geological formations.
- Covered in spiral carvings that seem to shift when you're not looking at them directly (she insists this is an optical illusion but doesn't sound convinced)
- Radiates cold — not thermal cold, but something she can only describe as "the absence of warmth as a concept"
- She believes it's a seal or containment device, not a power source

### The Water
- The flooded chamber beneath the altar has water that behaves abnormally
- No ripples, no current despite tidal connections
- Objects dropped in sink without displacement effects
- She took a sample. It evaporated within an hour despite being in a sealed vial.

### The Inscriptions
- She couldn't fully translate them — they're in a language that predates any she knows
- Key phrases she managed: "patient tide," "the dreaming deep," "do not wake"
- They made her physically uncomfortable. Headaches, tooth pain, a sense of being watched.

### The Undertow
- ~20 members, mostly clueless townspeople recruited with promises of prosperity
- Selen runs everything; the lieutenant (Cult Fanatic) is her only true believer
- They've been exploring the flooded chamber using water-breathing potions Lira brewed
- They haven't found what they're looking for yet, but Selen is getting desperate

### What Selen Wants
- Selen believes the altar can be "activated" to restore Thornhaven's fortunes
- Lira thinks Selen is wrong — "She's not trying to turn a key. She's trying to open a cage."
- The waterlogged tome Selen found describes a ritual. Lira thinks it's a containment ritual that Selen is reading backwards.

## After the Adventure

If the adventure resolves peacefully:
- Lira offers the player **2 Potions of Healing** and **1 custom potion** as thanks
- She'll stay in Thornhaven — "Someone needs to make sure that altar stays sealed. And the town needs an alchemist. Even if the town is full of idiots."
- She wants to study the altar from a safe distance. Sequel hook potential.

## Potions She Can Provide

| Potion | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Potion of Healing (x2) | Regain 2d4 + 2 HP |
| Lira's Custom Brew (x1) | **Potion of Clarity:** For 1 hour, advantage on WIS saving throws and immunity to the frightened condition. She brewed it specifically for "whatever's down in that cave." Pale gold liquid that smells like seawater and chamomile. |

She can also identify any potions the player has found (the water-breathing potions from the thugs, etc.) without needing a check.

## DM Notes

- Lira is the "exposition NPC" but she earns it through personality. Her information dumps should feel like a frustrated scientist ranting, not a quest-giver reading a briefing.
- The tonal shift happens through her. When the sarcasm drops and she talks about the altar, *that's* when the player should feel the genre change from comedy to genuine unease.
- She won't go to the caves. If asked: "Absolutely not. I've been in a basement for three weeks. I'm going to find actual food and a bed. You have fun with the eldritch horror." She'll stay with Voss if he's been freed, or go to the Salted Eel.
