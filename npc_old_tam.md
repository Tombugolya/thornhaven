# Old Tam — Fisherman
*Man who has seen everything and cares about nothing*

---

## At a Glance
- **Location:** The docks, always. He's fishing. He's always fishing.
- **Role:** The oracle nobody listens to. He tells the truth freely; nobody believes him because it's too easy.
- **Combat:** None. Old Tam is not going to fight anyone. He's fishing.
- **Tone:** Flat, unbothered, cosmically indifferent. He has seen the abyss, and the abyss looked boring.

---

## No Combat Stats

Old Tam is a Commoner (AC 10, HP 4). He will not fight. If combat occurs near him, he moves his chair slightly to the left and continues fishing.

---

## Personality & Voice

Old Tam is somewhere between 70 and 300 years old. Nobody is sure. He's been fishing at the Thornhaven docks for longer than most residents have been alive. He's seen everything, been surprised by nothing, and has the emotional range of a moderately interested rock.

He is the only honest person in Thornhaven. He will tell the player *everything they need to know* in the most casual, disinterested way possible. The joke is that the truth is freely available — nobody asks Tam because they assume anything that easy can't be real information.

**Voice:** Monotone, unhurried, slightly gravelly. He speaks the way you'd read a grocery list. Earth-shattering revelations get the same intonation as comments about bait.

**Key trait:** He does not care about drama. The town's conspiracy, the secret society, the eldritch horror — it's all just weather to Tam. Things happen. He fishes.

### Sample Lines

**When first approached:**
- "Oh aye, they've got a secret society. The Undertow, they call themselves. Meet on Tuesdays. Selen runs it. Terrible name if you ask me. Can I go back to my fish?"
- *(if the player expresses disbelief)* "Believe me, don't believe me. Fish don't care either way."
- "You're here about Voss? He's dead." *(beat)* "ILL. I meant ill. Those words sound alike." *(they don't)*

**If asked follow-up questions:**
- "The Saltworks. That's where they meet. Tuesdays. I said this already."
- "The caves? Aye. Bad business down there. Always has been. My granddad said the same thing. His granddad too, probably."
- "Selen thinks she's found something. She has. She just doesn't know what it is. Nobody ever does."
- "The alchemist girl, Lira? She didn't leave town. She's too angry to leave town. She's probably in a basement somewhere."
- *(about Edric)* "The Ashwick boy? He's in the Undertow. Nice lad. Bit dim. Don't tell his sister."

**Deep lore (if the player sits with him and asks the right questions):**
- "I saw something in the water. Thirty years ago. Out past the reef. Something big. Bigger than a whale. Moved different, too. Whales move like they're going somewhere. This moved like it was *waiting*."
- *(if pressed)* "I don't elaborate. I saw it. It saw me. We agreed to leave each other alone. Been fine since."
- "The founders knew. Why d'you think they built a town here? Wasn't for the fish. Well — was a bit for the fish."

**During the resolution (Session 3):**
- *(brings fresh fish, says nothing. It's the most touching thing in the entire campaign.)*
- *(if asked about the waterlogged tome and other sealed sites)* "Aye. There's more." *(goes back to his fish)*

## Role in the Story

Tam serves three functions:

1. **The Ironic Oracle.** All the information the player needs is available for free from a bored fisherman at the docks. The mystery isn't that the truth is hidden — it's that nobody thinks the truth could be this accessible. The player's own disbelief is the obstacle.

2. **The Failsafe.** If the player gets stuck, Tam is the ultimate backup. Maren can direct the player to him: "Have you talked to Old Tam? He sees everything from those docks." Tam will just... tell them whatever they need to know.

3. **The Emotional Anchor.** In the resolution, Tam brings fish and says nothing. After a campaign of words — lies, deflections, monologues, arguments — Tam's silence is the most eloquent moment. He's glad the player is okay. He shows it by bringing fish. That's Thornhaven.

---

## DM Tips

- **Play the deadpan straight.** The humor comes from the contrast between the enormity of what he's saying and the absolute flatness of his delivery. He should sound like he's discussing bait selection, even when describing eldritch sea creatures.
- **Don't make the player work for it.** Tam doesn't gatekeep. No checks required. He just *tells* them. The comedy is that it's too easy, not that it's hard.
- **The "Can I go back to my fish?" energy.** Every interaction should end with Tam trying to return to fishing. He's not rude — he's just done. He's said his piece. Fish are waiting.
- **The 30-year-old sighting.** Don't elaborate on what he saw. Ever. "I don't elaborate" is the line, and it's the one moment Tam says something that implies he might actually care. The restraint makes it more powerful than any description could.
- **The fish in Session 3.** This is the payoff. Set it up by having Tam be distant and uninvested throughout. When he brings fish at the end — no words, just fish — it should land with the weight of everything he didn't say. If the player doesn't react, that's fine. If they do, Tam nods and goes back to fishing.
