# Selen Dray — Harbormaster of Thornhaven
*The main antagonist. PTA president who committed crimes.*

---

## At a Glance
- **Used in:** Altar Chamber confrontation (Session 2, Part 2)
- **Role:** The villain — but a sympathetic one. She's a civic leader who went too far.
- **Stat Block:** Bandit Captain
- **Tone:** Not cackling evil. Disappointed manager energy. She genuinely believes she's saving Thornhaven.

**Multiattack.** Selen makes three melee attacks: two with her cutlass and one with her dagger. Or she makes two ranged attacks with her daggers.

## Personality & Voice

Selen is the most dangerous kind of antagonist: one who's *right* about the problem and *wrong* about the solution. Thornhaven IS dying. The fish ARE gone. She HAS been the only one willing to act. She's just chosen to act by kidnapping the magistrate and starting a secret society, which is... not great.

**Voice:** Controlled, authoritative, slightly weary. She speaks like someone running a town council meeting, even in combat. She doesn't raise her voice — she raises her expectations.

**Key trait:** She treats opposition like a personnel management problem. You're not her enemy; you're a difficult employee who needs to be brought on board or let go.

### Sample Lines

**Before combat:**
- "Thornhaven is dying. The fish are gone. The trade routes moved south. In five years this will be a ruin. What's in these caves could change that. Voss wanted to pretend the problem didn't exist. I chose to *act*."
- "Help me. Share in this. You could be a hero."
- "I didn't want it to come to this. I never wanted *any* of this. But someone had to do something."
- "You think I'm the villain here? I'm the only one who cared enough to try."

**During combat:**
- *(sighs like a disappointed manager)* "Fine. We'll do this the hard way."
- "I've run this harbor for fifteen years. I've faced worse than you." *(she hasn't, but she believes it)*
- *(to Edric)* "Hold the line, boy. Don't make me regret bringing you in."
- *(to the thugs)* "Stand your ground. This is what we trained for." *(they did not train for this)*

**After combat (if alive):**
- "I really thought it would work."
- "Just... make sure the town gets help. That part was real. We're dying out here."
- *(if asked about Voss)* "I didn't hurt him. I'm not a monster. I just... couldn't let him stop me."

## Combat Tactics

Selen is the toughest fight in the campaign. She's smart, experienced, and fights like someone used to commanding.

### Strategy
1. **Round 1:** She offers the deal first. Combat only starts if refused. When it does, she opens with her full Multiattack (3 attacks) against the biggest threat.
2. **Round 2+:** Uses the altar for **half cover** (+2 AC, effective AC 17). Kicks enemies toward the water (improvised shove: Athletics +4 vs. Athletics/Acrobatics).
3. **Parry:** Uses her reaction every round if targeted in melee (effective AC 17 once per round).
4. **Uses the thugs:** Positions so her allies can benefit from flanking. She's a leader — she doesn't fight alone.
5. **Uses the terrain:** Kicks toward the waist-deep water (difficult terrain). Tries to separate the PC from Maren.

### Morale
- Selen does NOT flee. She believes in what she's doing.
- She surrenders only if Edric goes down and she's below 20 HP. Even then, it's dignified: "Enough. This is over."
- If Edric surrenders first, she's shaken (disadvantage on her next attack) but keeps fighting.

### Terrain Reminder
- **Waist-deep water:** Difficult terrain in half the chamber.
- **Altar:** Half cover (+2 AC, +2 DEX saves) for anyone behind it.

## What She Knows

Selen knows everything about the conspiracy:

- She discovered the sea caves while inspecting the harbor for structural damage
- The altar was buried under centuries of sediment. She uncovered it with the Undertow's help.
- The inscriptions describe something sealed beneath the flooded chamber — she believes it's a source of power connected to the sea itself
- She forced Lira to brew water-breathing potions to explore the flooded sections
- Voss found out and tried to stop her. She had him chained in the caves rather than harmed. "He gets meals. He has a blanket. It's not *inhumane*."
- She recruited ~20 townspeople. Most of them have no idea what the actual plan is.
- The waterlogged tome she found describes other sites along the coast — Thornhaven is one of several seals.

**What she WON'T say (but is true):** She's scared. The altar does things she can't explain. The water moves on its own. She heard something once — not words, but intention. She's in over her head and too proud to admit it.

## Resolution Options

After the fight, the player decides Selen's fate. There is no "right" answer.

| Option | Advocate | Consequence |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Turn her over to Lord Aldenmere | Voss | She'll face formal justice. Probably imprisonment. The town loses any sympathy from the lord. |
| Let Thornhaven decide | Maren | Community justice. Messy, but the town gets to process what happened. Selen might get exile. |
| Let her go | No one (player's choice) | She's broken. She won't try again. But is letting a kidnapper walk the right call? |

Selen's last request regardless: "Just... make sure the town gets help. That part was real. We're dying out here."
