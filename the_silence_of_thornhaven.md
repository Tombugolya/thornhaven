# The Silence of Thornhaven
### A 5e Duet Mystery Campaign (2-3 Sessions, Levels 3-5)

*One player. One DM. Two sets of dice. A town full of terrible liars.*

---

## Overview

The player arrives in **Thornhaven**, a once-prosperous fishing village on a rocky coast, to investigate why the town's magistrate has stopped responding to letters. What should be a simple errand turns into a mystery involving a bumbling secret society, an alchemist with strong opinions, and something genuinely unsettling stirring beneath the cliffs.

**Tone:** Whimsical mystery with heart. The NPCs are endearing idiots. The conspiracy is held together with string and denial. But the thing in the caves? That part's real, and that's what makes it work — the comedy makes the genuine moments land harder.

Think: a town full of people who are *catastrophically bad* at keeping a secret, hiding something that actually deserves to be kept secret.

---

## Before Session 1

### Player Character Recommendations
Any class works. Investigative classes (Rogue, Bard, Ranger) get more from skill checks, but a Barbarian angrily interrogating nervous fishermen is equally funny.

### The Sidekick: Maren Ashwick
**Role:** The player's local contact — a Thornhaven native assigned to meet them.

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| **Race/Class** | Human, Warrior Sidekick (Tasha's) or simplified Fighter |
| **Level** | Same as PC |
| **Personality** | Deadpan, long-suffering, treats the town's collective nonsense like a parent dealing with toddlers |
| **Catchphrase energy** | *[sighing]* "Yes, that's Thornhaven for you." |
| **Secret** | Her brother Edric is in the secret society (she suspects but is in denial) |

**How to run Maren:** She's the straight man. The town is absurd; she reacts to it with exhausted fondness. She doesn't solve puzzles but will mutter helpful observations under her breath ("Oh, Berta's doing the thing where she talks about weather for nine minutes. She's hiding something.").

> Full stat block: see **`npc_maren_ashwick.md`**

## Session 1: "Nobody Here Is Acting Suspicious" (~2 hours)

### Opening Hook
> *The coastal road ends at a weathered signpost: THORNHAVEN — 1 MILE. The salt wind carries the smell of rotting kelp and something that might be bread, if someone were very bad at baking bread. You were sent by Lord Aldenmere to check on Magistrate Harlan Voss, who hasn't responded to three letters. Simple errand. Quick trip. The kind of job they give to people they don't want around the office for a week.*

The player arrives at dusk. Thornhaven is quiet — conspicuously, performatively quiet. Two fishermen see the player and immediately start whistling and looking at the sky.

### The Town's Terrible Secret-Keeping

The entire town knows about the Undertow (their secret society). They are *abysmal* at hiding it.

**Running gag:** Every NPC the player talks to uses the exact same cover story — "The magistrate? Oh, he's ill. Very ill. Terribly ill." — delivered with varying degrees of conviction. One of them (Old Tam) just says "He's dead" then immediately corrects himself: "ILL. I meant ill. Those words sound alike."

### Key Locations & NPCs

#### 1. The Salted Eel (tavern/inn)
- **Berta Gruun** — the innkeeper. Talks about the weather with manic, desperate energy when asked about anything else. If the player mentions Voss, she pivots to a 4-minute monologue about cloud formations. She's terrified but also genuinely believes she's being subtle.
- **Clue:** A torn journal page wedged under a floorboard in the player's room: *"...the tides are wrong. V. knows. He's been down to the caves three times this week. I told Lira but she said to keep quiet or—"* (torn). Someone has written in different handwriting below it: *"Berta, this is YOUR room, stop leaving these lying around."*

> Full NPC details: see **`npc_berta_gruun.md`**

#### 2. Magistrate's Manor (locked)
- Front door is locked. A note is pinned to it in shaky handwriting: "GONE AWAY. VERY ILL. DO NOT ENTER. DEFINITELY NOT KIDNAPPED."
- If they get in (DC 15 Thieves' Tools or DC 18 Strength): The house looks like someone left mid-sentence. Half-eaten meal, a letter to Lord Aldenmere that reads *"My Lord, I write to warn you of a grave matter concerning the—"* and stops. Ink everywhere.
- **Clue:** A drawer holds a hand-drawn cave map with one chamber circled and labeled "DO NOT" (rest smudged).

#### 3. The Docks
- **Old Tam** — elderly fisherman, the only honest person in town, but he delivers truth with such casual disinterest that it sounds made up. "Oh aye, they've got a secret society. The Undertow, they call themselves. Meet on Tuesdays. Selen runs it. Terrible name if you ask me. Can I go back to my fish?"
- The player may not believe him because of *how easy* that was. That's the joke. The truth is freely available; no one thinks to just ask Old Tam.

> Full NPC details: see **`npc_old_tam.md`**

### Maren Introduction
Maren meets the player at the Salted Eel. She's annoyed — she's been waiting for weeks.

> "Welcome to Thornhaven. Yes, everyone is acting strange. No, they won't tell you why. They think they're being clever. They are not being clever."

She knows:
- Voss has been "ill" for two months (she uses aggressive air quotes)
- The alchemist **Lira Crenn** supposedly left town — "She 'left' the way the magistrate is 'ill.'"
- Her brother Edric has been "going for walks" at 2am. She has Opinions about this.

### Session 1 Combat: Night Encounter
If the player investigates at night, **2 Thugs** follow them — wearing dark cloaks with the spiral wave symbol. They are not stealthy. One of them sneezes. The other whispers "SHUT UP" loud enough to echo off the cliffs.

They attack if spotted, but fight with the energy of people who signed up for a secret society and did NOT expect actual confrontation.

- If captured (DC 12 Intimidation — they crack easily): "Look, I just joined for the meetings. Selen said there'd be gold. There has not been gold. But you should still leave! The Undertow is... very dangerous!" *(he does not sound convinced)*
- **Loot:** 8 sp each, a vial of **pale blue liquid** (potion of water breathing), and a hand-drawn pamphlet titled "UNDERTOW MEMBER HANDBOOK" with rules like "1. Don't talk about the Undertow. 2. SERIOUSLY don't talk about the Undertow. 3. Tuesday meetings are MANDATORY."

> Thug stat block: see **`npc_thug.md`**

### Session 1 Goal
By the end, the player should:
- Know something is very wrong (and very funny) in Thornhaven
- Have leads pointing to the Saltworks, the caves, and Selen Dray
- Be thoroughly charmed by how bad this town is at conspiracy

## Session 2: "It's Not a Cult, It's a Community Organization" (~2-3 hours)

This is the big session — investigation, rescue, and the cave climax.

### Part 1: Finding Lira (~45 min)

**Lira Crenn** (the alchemist) is being held in the basement of the **Old Saltworks**.

Getting there:
- Asking around (DC 12 Persuasion) → a dock worker says "Oh, the Saltworks? That's where the cul— the COMMUNITY ORGANIZATION meets."
- Following an Undertow member at night (DC 14 Stealth, but DC 10 because they keep tripping over things)
- Maren: "I bet it's the Saltworks. Everything shady in this town happens at the Saltworks. We had a rat problem there once. Also a smuggling ring. Also a very aggressive book club."

**The Saltworks:**
- Ground floor: dusty, abandoned. A trapdoor (DC 12 Perception) leads down.
- Basement: a meeting room with a table, chairs, minutes from previous meetings (agenda items include "1. The Plan, 2. Snack rotation dispute, 3. Todd keeps telling his wife about meetings — FINAL WARNING TODD"), and a locked room (DC 13) holding **Lira**.

> Full NPC details: see **`npc_lira_crenn.md`**

**Lira Crenn** — mid-30s, furious, sarcastic:
> "Oh wonderful, a rescue. Only three weeks late. I've been subsisting on salt crackers and resentment."

What she knows:
- **Selen Dray** (the harbormaster) runs the Undertow. She found something in the sea caves — an ancient stone altar. It's real, it's old, and it radiates cold.
- Lira was forced to brew water-breathing potions so they could explore a flooded chamber beneath the altar.
- "Harlan tried to stop them. He's either in the caves or... look, Selen isn't violent. She's just *aggressively delusional* about her importance."
- **The serious part:** "But the thing in the caves? That's not a joke. The water moves on its own down there. The inscriptions... I couldn't read them, but they made my teeth ache. Whatever's sealed down there was sealed for a reason."

**Combat — Saltworks Rescue:**
- **3 Thugs** + **1 Cult Fanatic** (reflavored as Undertow lieutenant — the only competent one)
- One thug is mid-sandwich when the fight starts and fights one-handed for round 1
- The Cult Fanatic is genuinely dangerous — this is where the tone shifts slightly. She's a true believer.

> Stat blocks: see **`npc_thug.md`** and **`npc_cult_fanatic.md`**

### Part 2: The Caves (~1-1.5 hours)

The sea caves are accessible at low tide along the northern cliffs. The player has Voss's map.

**Cave Map:**

```
ENTRANCE (tidal, wet, slippery)
    |
PASSAGE (60ft, bioluminescent algae)
    |
FORK ──── LEFT: Dead end. Voss is here.
    |
SLOPE DOWN (gets cold, carved symbols)
    |
ALTAR CHAMBER (large, partially flooded)
```

**The Fork — Left:**
**Magistrate Harlan Voss** is chained to the wall. Alive, exhausted, but maintaining a dignified fury.
> "I have been chained to a rock for *six weeks* by a woman who runs a fish-counting office. Do you have any idea how humiliating that is?"

He knows the history: "Thornhaven was built here to keep people *away* from what's below. The founders sealed that chamber. Selen thinks she's found treasure. She's found a lock and she's trying to open it."

> Full NPC details: see **`npc_harlan_voss.md`**

**The Altar Chamber:**
The comedy fades here. This room should feel *different*.

- 40ft cavern, half-flooded. The water is unnaturally still — no ripples even when disturbed.
- A **black stone altar** covered in spiral carvings. It radiates cold that feels like loneliness.
- **Selen Dray** is here with **2 Thugs** and **Edric Ashwick** (Maren's brother — the reveal).

### The Confrontation

Selen is pouring potions over the altar, reading from a waterlogged tome. She's not a cackling villain — she's a civic leader who genuinely believes she's saving her town and has gone too far to stop.

> "Thornhaven is dying. The fish are gone. The trade routes moved south. In five years this will be a ruin. What's in these caves could change that. Voss wanted to pretend the problem didn't exist. I chose to *act*."

Key beats:
1. **Selen offers a deal.** "Help me. Share in this. You could be a hero."
2. **Edric sees Maren.** He's young, earnest, and immediately looks like he wants to disappear. "Maren, I can explain—" / Maren: "Can you? Can you *really*?"
3. **If refused:** Selen sighs like a disappointed manager and orders the attack.

**Combat — Altar Chamber:**

| Enemy | Stat Block | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| Selen Dray | Bandit Captain | Fights smart — uses altar for cover, kicks enemies toward water |
| 2 Thugs | Thug | One of them clearly does not want to be here |
| Edric | Guard | Surrenders below half HP, especially if Maren talks to him |

> Full stat blocks: see **`npc_selen_dray.md`**, **`npc_thug.md`**, **`npc_edric_ashwick.md`**

**Terrain:** Waist-deep water = difficult terrain in half the room. The altar provides half cover.

**Maren's Conflict:** She doesn't attack in round 1 (shocked). The player can talk Edric down mid-fight (Action: DC 13 Persuasion, advantage if invoking Maren).

**The reluctant thug:** One of the thugs has been questioning his life choices. If the player addresses him directly (any Persuasion check): "You know what, you're right. I'm done. I only joined because Todd said there'd be gold." He sits down.

### After the Fight

The altar goes dormant once the ritual is interrupted. The water recedes. The cold fades to a chill, then nothing.

Selen, if alive, is deflated rather than defiant: "I really thought it would work."

## Session 3 (Optional/Short): "The Quiet After" (~1 hour)

If you want a third session for resolution, or fold this into the end of Session 2.

### Resolutions

**Selen Dray:**
- Captured: The player decides her fate. Turn her over to Lord Aldenmere? Let Thornhaven decide? Maren wants local justice. Voss wants the law. Neither is wrong.
- Selen's last request: "Just... make sure the town gets help. That part was real. We're dying out here."

**Edric Ashwick:**
- Maren asks the player to advocate for leniency. "He's an idiot, but he's *my* idiot."
- Genuine moral choice. No right answer.

**Voss:**
- Recovered, grateful, writing a very strongly worded letter to Lord Aldenmere about "inadequate response times."
- Asks the player to help **seal the cave entrance** — a skill challenge (3 successes before 2 failures, DC 13, any relevant skill/tool).
- Reward: **Letter of commendation**, 100 gp, offer of a house in Thornhaven.

**The Town:**
- Old Tam brings fresh fish and says nothing. It's the most touching thing that happens in the entire campaign.
- Berta apologizes while somehow still talking about the weather. "I'm sorry I lied. It's supposed to rain Thursday."
- Todd's wife comes up to the player: "I KNEW it was a cult."

### Ending

> *The road out of Thornhaven is warmer than the road in. Maren walks you to the signpost. "You could stay," she says. Then: "But I'd have to find you housing, and there's paperwork, and honestly I've had enough paperwork for a lifetime." She hands you a carved wooden token — a wave, but not a spiral. "Don't be a stranger." Behind you, you can hear Berta shouting about a lovely sunset. The fishing boats are out again. All eight of them.*

### If the Player Gets Stuck
The mystery should never stall. Failsafe order:
1. **Maren says something dry** ("I wonder what's in the Saltworks. Besides salt. And crime.")
2. **An NPC caves** (Berta slips a note, Old Tam just tells them everything again)
3. **The Undertow acts** (another clumsy attack, the player witnesses them moving cargo while loudly whispering)

**Every important clue is available in at least 3 ways.**

### Loot Summary
| Source | Reward |
|--------|--------|
| Undertow thugs | 8 sp each, water breathing potions, the handbook |
| Saltworks | 25 gp lockbox, coded notes (code is just the alphabet shifted by 1), cave maps |
| Selen Dray | Fine cutlass (50 gp), 40 gp, the waterlogged tome (sequel hook) |
| Voss | Letter of commendation, 100 gp, house offer |
| Lira | 2 Potions of Healing + 1 custom potion |

### Sequel Hook
The waterlogged tome references other sealed sites along the coast. Thornhaven was just one lock. Old Tam, if asked about it after everything, just looks at the sea and says: "Aye. There's more." And goes back to his fish.

## Tips for Running This Duet

1. **Voice the NPCs.** Even badly. Especially badly. Berta's weather panic, Tam's monotone, Voss's outrage — these make the world feel alive.
2. **Let the player drive.** Maren follows. The NPCs react. The player decides the pace.
3. **The tonal shift matters.** Sessions 1 and early Session 2 are funny. The cave should feel genuinely eerie. The contrast is what makes both work.
4. **Don't over-plan combat.** With one player, fights are fast. 3-4 rounds max. If it drags, have an enemy flee or surrender.
5. **The moral choices are the real climax.** What happens to Selen and Edric matters more than the altar. Give the player time to think.
